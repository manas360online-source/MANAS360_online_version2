1 Definitions
Clause 1.1

Data Fiduciary

As defined under DPDPA 2023 — refers to MANAS360, which determines the purpose and means of processing personal data.

Clause 1.2

Data Processor

The Provider, who processes personal data on behalf of the Data Fiduciary in the course of providing therapeutic services.

Clause 1.3

Personal Data

Any data about an identifiable individual, including health data, as defined under DPDPA 2023.

Clause 1.4

Processing

Includes collection, recording, storage, organisation, structuring, use, sharing, disclosure, restriction, erasure, or destruction of Personal Data.

2 Scope
2.1 This DPA governs the Provider's processing of patient personal data accessed through the MANAS360 Platform in connection with the delivery of mental health services.
3 Processing Obligations
3.1 The Provider shall process patient data ONLY for the purpose of providing mental health services through the Platform.
3.2 The Provider shall NOT process patient data for any other purpose, including personal research, marketing, or sharing with third parties.
3.3 The Provider shall implement the following mandatory security safeguards:
🔒Use a secure, password-protected device for all Platform access
📱Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on the Provider account
🖥️Not access patient data on shared or public computers
🔇Conduct sessions from a private, sound-proof location
3.4 The Provider shall adhere to the principle of data minimisation — accessing only such Personal Data as is strictly necessary for patients formally assigned to them. The Provider is strictly prohibited from downloading, exporting, copying, transferring, or storing any Personal Data outside the Platform's secure environment unless expressly permitted in writing by MANAS360 or required under applicable law.
🚫 The Provider shall not attempt to access, view, retrieve, or manipulate personal or clinical data relating to patients who are not under their authorised care or clinical responsibility.
4 Sub-Processing
4.1 The Provider shall not appoint or engage any sub-processor to process patient data without obtaining prior written consent from MANAS360.
⚠️ Any sub-processing carried out without prior written authorisation from MANAS360 shall constitute a material breach of this Agreement and may result in immediate termination and legal action.
5 Data Breach
🚨
Clause 5.1 — Notification Obligation
Notify MANAS360 within 12 hours

The Provider shall notify MANAS360 of any suspected or actual personal data breach within 12 hours of becoming aware.

📋
Clause 5.2 — Notification Contents
Required notification details

Notification must include all of the following:

🔍Nature of the breach
📂Categories of data affected
👤Number of individuals affected
🛡️Remedial measures taken
⚖️
Clause 5.3 — Provider Liability
Sole liability for breach consequences

The Provider shall be solely liable for all losses, damages, penalties, regulatory fines, or adverse consequences arising from: unauthorised/unlawful processing; negligent data handling; failure to implement required safeguards; or delay in mandatory breach notification.

6 Data Subject Requests
6.1 If a patient contacts the Provider directly to exercise their DPDPA rights (access, correction, erasure), the Provider shall forward the request to MANAS360 within 24 hours. The Provider shall assist MANAS360 in fulfilling all statutory obligations relating to data subject rights.
ℹ️ DPDPA Rights include: Right to Access · Right to Correction · Right to Erasure · Right to Nominate · Right to Grievance Redressal. Providers must not attempt to fulfil these requests independently — all requests must be routed to MANAS360.
7 Return and Deletion
7.1 Upon termination of the Provider relationship, the Provider shall cease all processing immediately and confirm in writing to MANAS360 that no patient data is retained on any personal devices, local storage, or external systems.
✅ Written confirmation of data deletion must be submitted to MANAS360 within 7 days of termination.
8 Audit
🔎

MANAS360 Data Protection Audit Rights

📅 Max once per quarter ⏱ 7 days' prior notice 📄 Full documentation access

MANAS360 reserves the right to conduct data protection audits to verify compliance. The Provider shall cooperate fully and provide reasonable access to all relevant documentation demonstrating compliance with this DPA and applicable data protection laws.

9 Governing Law
9.1 This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of India. Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement shall be referred to and finally resolved by arbitration under the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996. The seat and venue of arbitration shall be Bengaluru, Karnataka, India, and proceedings shall be conducted in the English language.
